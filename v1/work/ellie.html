In Fall 2016, I took a course at UC Berkeley called "Critical Practices" taught by Professor
Jill Miller
. Each group was asked to create an object to "provoke" its audience, be it eliciting an emotion, thinking critically or otherwise. Since the assignment coincided with the aftermath of the 2016 Presidential Election, our group was very focused on improving interpersonal communication. In particular, we wanted to help complete strangers engage with each other in a comfortable discussion. To do this, we created Ellie the Jellyfish as an approachable and silly way for people to start conversations with each other. Who can resist an adorable invertebrate that just wants to talk fish philosophy?
