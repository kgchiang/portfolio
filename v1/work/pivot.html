In Fall 2015, as a member of Berkeley Innovation, a human-centered design student group at UC Berkeley, my team worked with
Vertigo Software
 (now MLBAM) to design and prototype a version control tool for designers (think GitHub for design files). We conducted user research to gauge the workflow of various designers, as well as the pros and cons of git to aid us in our ideation process. Each member took ownership of different parts of the prototype, and I was responsible for the interface of the user feed and file history. We created a few different lo-fi and mid-fi prototypes, and received feedback from the team at Vertigo teach time, and all of our efforts culminated in Pivot.
