In Fall 2016, I took a course at UC Berkeley called "Critical Practices" taught by Professor
Jill Miller
. Each group was asked to create a "hyper-personal prototype" - an object made with only the needs of a single unique user in mind. The subject for our prototype was Christopher Hunn (pictured above), an advisor at the Computer Science department at the time. I conducted two interviews with Chris, and through them I learned that he loves to listen to and tell stories; he is also an avid writer who regularly attends "The Moth", a weekly gathering for storytellers. Chris hopes to read his stories on the stage of "The Moth" someday, but has not had the chance to do so. Our group wanted to create an object to provide a space for Chris to tell his stories to his very own audience, and thus Chris.0 was born.
