In Fall 2016, I took a course at UC Berkeley called "Reimagining Mobility", taught by Professor Rob Hennigar. Each group was tasked with creating a prototype based on our notions of "mobility" and how it will transform twenty to thirty years from now. My team decided to focus on user experience for autonomous vehicle ride-sharing, namely trying to answer the question "how does one take comfort on the go?". After a few iterations of the design cycle, we arrived at a conclusion: our solution is a service called "vroom" - a way for clients to customize the interior of a vehicle through the mobile interface so that when their ride shows up, everything is set up just the way they like, and they can enjoy a comfortable ride to their destination.
